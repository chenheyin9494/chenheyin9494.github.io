I am a PhD candidate in Agricultural and Resource Economics at UC Davis, specializing in applied econometrics with a focus on marine and environmental resource management. My dissertation investigates how economic incentives shape the behavior of fishing fleets and charter firms under regulatory and market constraints.

My methodological toolkit centers on reduced-form causal identification — including Cragg's double hurdle models, Heckman selection models, panel fixed/mixed effects, difference-in-differences, synthetic control, and nested logit — applied to large-scale administrative datasets including NOAA Vessel Monitoring System (VMS) GPS tracking records, commercial fishing logbooks, and charter operator data.

Beyond fisheries, I have contributed to research on COVID-19 vaccine effectiveness in California prisons, the causal impacts of property rights in common pool resource management, and food systems resilience at the World Bank.

Research

Dissertation & Projects

Fuel Price Shocks and Fishing Fleet Behavior in the Atlantic HMS Pelagic Longline Fishery

Dissertation Chapter 1. Uses Cragg's double hurdle model on 16,000+ vessel-trip panel data (2013–2019) from the Atlantic highly migratory species fishery. Merges 1M+ VMS GPS records with commercial logbook data across 111 ports to estimate fuel price elasticities for trip participation (–0.57 to –0.82) and fishing intensity (–0.13 to –0.23). Documents heterogeneous effects by vessel size.

Double Hurdle Model VMS/Logbook Data Fixed Effects Heckman Selection

Charter Fishing Firm Operational Decisions in Alaska

Dissertation Chapter 2. Examines Alaska charter halibut fishing firm behavior under IPHC regulatory constraints (Regions 2C and 3A, Catch Sharing Plan). Uses nested logit models to analyze participation and effort decisions (2005–2015), capturing how firms respond to bag limits, season restrictions, and quota policy changes.

Nested Logit Discrete Choice Charter Fisheries IPHC Regulations

Private Property Rights and Common Pool Resource Management

Applies reduced-form causal analysis (DiD and Synthetic Control) to evaluate the effects of catch shares on marine fisheries status using 50+ years of panel data covering 170 exclusive economic zones and 800+ species. Estimates marginal and average treatment effects using parametric and semiparametric methods with selection on unobservables.

Diff-in-Diff Synthetic Control MTE RAM Legacy DB

COVID-19 Vaccine Effectiveness in California State Prisons

Estimated vaccine treatment effects using Cox proportional hazards models on person-day panel data covering 60,000+ individuals across 35 California prisons, finding effectiveness of 74% (first dose) and 97% (second dose). Applied quasi-experimental design with time-varying covariates to address selection bias and facility-level clustering.
